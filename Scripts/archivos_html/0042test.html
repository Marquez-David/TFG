Zach Parise heating up, scores twice as Wild beat Sabres 4-1

BUFFALO, N.Y. — Hours before Zach Parise’s two-goal performance Tuesday, Wild coach Bruce Boudreau expressed confidence in the veteran winger, saying he always finds a way to start scoring.

“It takes a little longer now, but he’s playing well now, he’s getting a little bit of confidence back,” Boudreau said before the Wild’s 4-1 win over the Sabres in front of 15,522 in KeyBank Center. “That goes a long way.”

Right now, having scored four times during a three-game streak, Parise is brimming with confidence.

Parise’s first-period goals – he scored at 7:14 and on the power play at 19:56 – gave the Wild a commanding two-goal lead they wouldn’t relinquish.

For good measure, Parise blocked a shot in the waning seconds of the third period.

“That’s perfect,” Boudreau said. “It’s the mark of a true competitor doing things for the team.”

Barely a week ago, Parise, 35, had scored just once in a 10-game stretch. Wild defenseman Ryan Suter, who joked about Parise’s penchant for changing sticks, could see the frustration on his friend’s face.

“He’s used to scoring goals, getting points,” he said.

But Parise has quickly turned around a tepid start in which he scored just three four times in his first 18 games.

“You can go either way,” Parise said. “I was going the other way for a little while. But I was able to, I guess, work my way out of it.”

Parise opened the scoring by stripping Sabres center Casey Mittelstadt in the neutral zone and burying Joel Eriksson Ek’s feed on a two-on-one.

He tipped Suter’s shot in later, a dagger for the Sabres.

“He’s starting to feel it a little bit again,” Boudreau said. “And we need those goals.”

The Wild still rank at the bottom of the Central Division. Boudreau has been on his players to avoid looking at the standings.

“It’s hard not to, though,” Suter acknowledged. “We know where we are, we know what we got to do to get out of it, and I thought tonight we took a good step forward.”

Boudreau gave his players a pep talk Tuesday, telling them to “believe in yourselves” and that the Wild are a “good team.”

“You’ve beaten some really good teams this year,” he said he said he told them. “You’ve been in every game for the last 10 games. So if you start believing in yourself, then good things are going to happen.

“I said the same thing when I took over Washington for the first time, because I knew they were a good team but they just weren’t playing good.”

The Sabres, having lost eight of their nine 10 games following a scorching start, have been playing awfully. The Wild could sense their frustrations. In the second period, Sabres captain Jack Eichel went after Eriksson Ek and threw punches, although neither player received a fighting major.

“I didn’t really see him coming,” Eriksson Ek said. “He came off my back and, like, I just wasn’t ready for it.”

Parise said: “We’ve been there. You could tell we were maybe getting under their skin a little bit and not giving them a whole lot to feel good about.”

Eichel said he was “just trying to spark some energy.”

Meanwhile, Wild goalie Alex Stalock, who subbed for the struggling Devan Dubnyk, looked strong in his 10th outing this season, stopping 30 shots. Sabres defenseman Brandon Montour spoiled his shutout bid 17:46 into the third period.

Defenseman Jonas Brodin had scored earlier for the Wild before winger Jason Zucker’s empty-net goal sealed it.

The Wild lost defenseman Matt Dumba, who sat out the third period, to an undisclosed injury.

“I haven’t talked to the trainers at all,” Boudreau said.
